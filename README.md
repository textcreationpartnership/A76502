1. TO The Right Worshipful Sir William Blackett, Baronet;

#####Body#####
PSAL. LXV. 2.O Thou that hearest Prayer, unto Thee shall all Flesh come.GOD the Lord is every where 
    _ I. That it is our Duty to Pray.

      * On the contrary;

      * First, Pray humbly.

      * Secondly, Pray ardently.

    _ II. The Lord hears our Prayers.

      * On the contrary;

      * First, Pray humbly.

      * Secondly, Pray ardently.

    _ III. Prayer is a Special Part of God's Worship.

      * On the contrary;

      * First, Pray humbly.

      * Secondly, Pray ardently.

    _ IV. Of the Efficacy and Power of Prayer, which the best of Persons have used.

      * On the contrary;

      * First, Pray humbly.

      * Secondly, Pray ardently.

    _ V. The Object of our Prayers, GOD alone.

      * On the contrary;

      * First, Pray humbly.

      * Secondly, Pray ardently.

    _ VI. The Subject of our Prayers is Good and Evil; Good, for which we pray; Evil, against which we petition: the former of which is called Supplication, the later Deprecation.

      * On the contrary;

      * First, Pray humbly.

      * Secondly, Pray ardently.

    _ VII. The manner of our Prayers, humbly and ardently, in Faith and full Hope on God for the Fruits of them.

      * On the contrary;

      * First, Pray humbly.

      * Secondly, Pray ardently.
